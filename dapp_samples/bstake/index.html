Participate in the future of finance.*

bStake is DeFi designed for adoption: it’s fast, affordable, and easy to use. Swap between assets on the decentralized exchange, and create bStake Dollars (bsUSD) or supply liquidity to earn bStake Tokens.

Staked bStake gives you voting rights and a share of the network fees. Inflation decreases over time, so take part from the start and help shape the future of finance.

*bStake's goal is to become the future of finance, but this is a decentralized experiment: nothing is guaranteed. Use at your own risk.

Auto claim & stake.

To earn bStake staking rewards, swap bStake for sbStake, or deposit bStake as collateral. Your bStake is added to a staking pool and the rewards auto-claimed, so the value will increase over time.

Supply liquidity.

Earn bStake Tokens for supplying liquidity to an incentivized pool. Liquidity pools also receive 50% of the fees earned by trades within their pool.

Receive a fair share from launch.

bStake is a DAO, owned and operated by bStake Token holders. There was no bStake pre-mine, so from launch, every participant will have an equal opportunity to own a share of bStake and vote on its future.

To maximize your influence, participate early. bStake distributed 480,000 bStake a day for the first 60 days, and now tapers by 0.5% a day until it stabilizes at 1,250 (<2% inflation).

Participate in governance.

Have your say on the future of bStake. If you hold bStake Tokens, you can vote to add new collateral types, pay for initiatives from the DAO fund, adjust the fees and token economics, and more.

Join the discussion on the forum, then submit a proposal or vote on others’ – your level of involvement is up to you.

Audited for your security.

bStake is decentralized, and operates via smart contracts. The bStake smart contracts were audited by Slowmist, whose list of clients includes leading exchanges like Binance, Huobi, and OKex.2

An audit reduces the risk of smart contract bugs, but there’s always a risk. Invest only what you can afford to lose.
